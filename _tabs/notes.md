On this page, I set to combine my experiment with Zettelkasten with my open-source philosophy. Starting from August 1st, 2021, I make my way into notes that I can hopefully reuse.

I am the writer of these **notes**, **my future-self** is the audience. You will undoubtedly disagree with most of these notes or find them hard to read; this is a continuous work in progress. Nevertheless, you mind find **your way** and benefits, who knows?

Reluctant to new tools and their short lifetime, I start with the minimum, and I'll see along as requirements emerge. Familiar with [Jekyll](https://jekyllrb.com/) and [Github Page](https://pages.github.com/), I build on my website and rely on markdown files. I use **wiki links** to connect my notes, which I automatically map into **backlinks**. I list the notes without backlinks below as they are starting points.

I take a lot of inspiration from [Niklas Luhmann' Zettelkasten](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niklas_Luhmann), [Andy Matuschak' evergreen notes](https://notes.andymatuschak.org/About_these_notes), [Sönke Ahrens' book](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/34507927-how-to-take-smart-notes) and the [Soren Bjornstad's TiddlyWiki](https://zettelkasten.sorenbjornstad.com/).
