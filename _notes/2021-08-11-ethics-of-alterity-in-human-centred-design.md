[[steen-sthv-2015]] describes [[human-centred-design-hcd]] as a process to confront and construct ideas collaboratively. Thus, the ability to alter one's view or plan is critical.

He uses the ethics of alterity (work by Levinas and Derrida), which takes the other and the relationships between the other and self as a starting point.

He illustrates that it is challenging for designers (like everyone else) as they have their goals. So how to minimise the 'self' of designers getting in the way and let users' ideas influence the project?

It comes from a tendency to program innovation and to close towards decisions.
