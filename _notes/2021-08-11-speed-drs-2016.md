Design from, with, and by design provides the vocabulary on which tools and methods remain to develop.

Speed and Oberlander propose a framework to classify the role of data in design. It helps designers reflect on the role and value they can give to data and leverage data based on the challenge and data they have at the end.

From is when the data is an inspiration material, helping to measure something/someone behaviour in their context. Examples are technology and cultural probes, interviews, contextual mapping, observations.

With is when data supports the design process by connecting designers with the participants or the community.

By is when data drive the design process and generate design outputs.

They raise the dynamic nature of data, conflicting with the 'phase' of an approach like the double-diamond, as data will continue to flow.
