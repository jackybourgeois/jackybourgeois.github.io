HCD is an ISO standard of 1999 (ISO 9241-210) with four principles:

- the active involvement of potential users throughout the project
- the search for an appropriate balance of functions between people and technology
- the organisation of an iterative process of research, design and evaluation
- the organisation of multidisciplinary teamwork

HCD typically involves interviews and workshops [[steen-sthv-2015]].

Approaches such as Thing-Centred Design highlight the many valuable perspectives to capture (and being capture) as part of the design process. [[coulton-design-2019]] phrase it through the constellation metaphor where the multiple 'stars', elements of the new ecologies, can all bring their perspectives. These perspectives arise from the behavioural data that they capture. Thus, the HCD process no longer revolves around the single human, single potential user of the design outcome.

There is a distinction between HCD as a design process and HCD as a design solution. Here that might be a bridge to [[value-sensitive-design-vsd]] and the involvement of stakeholders.
